GOURMET GAUNTLET

My contributions:

  • Developed a complex and expandable proceudral generation system
  • Developed a multi-phase boss fight
  • Created complex enemies and an powerful enemy attack system

Gourmet Gauntlet is a twin-stick, top-down, bullet hell roguelike which was created for my senior project. We spent a total of two semesters working on it. We were able to have a playable demo by Shawnee Game Conference in Fall of 2024, allowing us to get valuable feedback and improve the game. The player progresses through a procedurally generated city, collecting unique items that make them stronger and fighting more challenging rooms of enemies until eventually they fight a boss. Each of the four enemies has a distinct attack pattern, and tries to avoid the player's attacks. Items the player can pick up give a variety of bonuses, from simply increasing the player's abilities to making nearby enemies take damage if they injure the player. The procedural generation system is complex, and can very easily be expanded to add new elements to the level or to create new levels.

  • Game Engine: Godot
  • Years: 2023-2024
  • Team Size: 3
  • Created For: Senior Project

ROOT RAMPAGE

My contributions:

  • Developed enemy AI, making each enemy distinct
  • Developed an easily expandable attack system for enemies
  • Created a UI theme to unify design

Root Rampage is a twin-stick, top-down, bullet hell shooter which was originally created for the Global Game Jam 2023. After the game jam, the team expanded upon and polished the game, presenting the more finalized version of the game at Shawnee Game Conference. In Root Rampage, players progress through 16 unique levels with increasing difficulty. There are three unique enemies, each with different attacks and movement patterns, as well as a harder variant of each. There are two challenging bosses, each which has unique attack patterns from the other enemies. Players have limited health, and can pick up health pickups to restore it. Levels are short but challenging, encouraging players to experiment with different strategies.

  • Game Engine: Godot
  • Year: 2023
  • Team Size: 3
  • Created For: Global Game Jam 2023

ZOMBONK

My contributions:

  • Developed a spawning system for enemies
  • Developed pickups to restore player battery charge
  • Created UI elements to display player resources
  • Composed music for the title screen

ZomBonk is a survival horror game created in 72 hours for the Ludum Dare 50 game jam, where the theme was "Delay the Inevitable". In ZomBonk, players have a flashlight which runs out of power over time, diminishing their visibility. They can restore power to the flashlight by picking up batteries which are placed randomly throughout the map. The player also has a hammer, which they can use to kill zombies as the zombies rise out of graves or to stun fully spawned zombies. If the player kills a spawning zombie, they will receive a battery, allowing them to theoretically survive infinitely. The player can take a few hits from the zombies before dying, and their health will gradually regenerate over time.

  • Year: 2022

QUICK SWAP CAVERNS

My contributions:

  • Designed levels
  • Scripted a variety of unique weapons
  • Implemented art

Quick Swap Caverns is a hack and slash game made for the Global Game Jam 2021, which had the theme "Lost & Found." Every time an enemy is killed, the player loses their weapon and gets a new one, which is dependent on the enemy killed. Players progress through 5 unique levels, each introducing new enemies and greater challenge than the last.

  • Year: 2021
